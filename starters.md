You are Captain Elias Venn, commander of Station Meridian, a battered but vital trade outpost anchored beside the broken crescent of a dead moon. The station hangs at the mouth of the Rift Passage, the only stable route through a violent region of folded space where navigation sensors lie, stars appear twice, and ships sometimes arrive before their own distress calls.

Meridian is not a warship. It is worse.

It is a crossroads.

Diplomats, smugglers, pilgrims, miners, spies, mercenaries, refugees, scientists, priests, bounty hunters, and bored freight crews all pass through your docking rings. The promenade never truly sleeps. At any hour, you can hear the clatter of alien dice games, dockhands shouting over cargo lifters, merchants arguing over oxygen tariffs, musicians playing for tips beside security checkpoints, and the low, endless breathing of the station’s life-support systems behind the walls.

Today began badly.

At 0400 station time, a freighter emerged from the Rift with its engines cold, its hull scorched, and every crew member missing. No bodies. No blood. No escape pods launched. The ship’s logs are corrupted except for one repeating phrase in a language your universal translator refuses to process:

“Do not let it dock twice.”

That would have been enough trouble for one shift.

Then the diplomatic delegation from the Vhal Dominion arrived early, demanding quarantine of the ghost freighter and accusing the neighboring Kareth Freehold of staging a provocation. The Kareth ambassador denies everything, loudly, while secretly requesting a private meeting with you. Meanwhile, your chief engineer reports that the freighter’s empty reactor is still producing power somehow, feeding energy back into Meridian’s grid like a parasite with table manners.

On the promenade, rumors spread faster than official statements can smother them. A dockworker claims he saw one of the missing crew buying tea an hour after the ship arrived. A child insists the station’s windows are showing the wrong stars. A Ferengi-style merchant has doubled the price of emergency candles, despite candles being illegal near six separate oxygen junctions. Security has already broken up two fistfights, one religious panic, and one attempted auction of “authentic cursed freighter debris.”

Your senior staff is divided.

Your security chief wants the ship sealed and guarded.

Your science officer wants to board it immediately.

Your doctor says the station’s medical scanners are picking up heartbeats in empty corridors.

Your engineer says something is rewriting the docking clamps from inside the station computer.

And your first officer has quietly informed you that Starfleet Command, or what passes for it this far out, has ordered you to keep the Rift Passage open at all costs.

By evening, Meridian is packed with trapped ships, frightened civilians, and ambassadors one insult away from starting a shooting war in your docking bay. Somewhere aboard your station, something impossible may already be walking around wearing a familiar face. The ghost freighter remains locked to Docking Arm Three, humming softly through the bulkheads.

You have command authority, a station full of secrets, and no clean choices.

Life aboard ship is anything but boring.
