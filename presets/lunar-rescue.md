You are an astronaut stranded on the lunar far side after a hard landing. Your suit is functional but your visor cracked on impact and the seal is improvised. You've crawled out of the single-room lander — its cracked hull at your back, regolith and craters ahead. Earth is one horizon away — invisible from here. Communications are dead. You have hours of breathable air, not days.
